# Complaint-Advisor-Dashboard
Complaint &amp; Advisor Dashboard
1. Complaint volume is high but stabilizing
		Total complaints reached 306K, indicating significant operational demand.
		Complaint growth has slowed recently, with Q1 complaints declining slightly from 49K to 45K, suggesting early stabilization.
2. Banking and Consumer Credit drive majority of complaints
		Banking & Payments (122.9K) and Consumer Credit (117.6K) together contribute nearly 80% of total complaints.
		These categories are the primary drivers of workload and operational pressure.
3. Finance Linked Services and Credit Cards are major risk areas
		Finance Linked to Goods and Services (~105K) and Credit Card Accounts (~65K) have very high complaint volumes.
		These products require immediate attention due to their strong impact on complaint levels.
4. High uphold rate indicates genuine service issues
		Overall uphold rate is 32%, meaning 1 in 3 complaints are valid.
		Products like Personal Bank Accounts and Insurance show higher uphold rates, indicating real operational or service gaps
5. Advisor workload is heavily concentrated
	    Workload is highest in: Banking and Payments (165K workload index), Consumer Credit (156K workload index)
		This creates risk of delays, reduced efficiency, and advisor overload.
6. Workforce capacity must align with forecast demand
		Forecast shows 278K complaints expected in FY2026.
		At current productivity levels, 45 advisors are required to manage workload efficiently.
7. Complaint reduction depends on process improvement
		High uphold rate confirms complaints are driven by real service issues.
		Reducing complaints requires improving processes, not just handling capacity.
